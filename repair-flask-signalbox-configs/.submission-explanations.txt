The last round still measured EASY (gpt5-5 5/5, opus 4/5), so this revision adds
a genuinely new interacting mechanism instead of more edge cases: snapshot and
rollback bulletin ops on a 63-row log. A rollback restores a captured lock map,
clears hold stacks on snapshotted tracks (so the later matching release must be
treated as a no-op), and rolls back witness_active bulletin ownership while
applied-row history stays intact. The maintenance-window tail interleaves these
with an amend, a hold, a junction replace, and a requires_when cascade wipe that
the handover rollback has to undo — agents that model rollback as a plain state
reset, keep the hold alive, or treat the rolled-back BUL-BJ replace as a live
witness land on the wrong lock set or a wrong goal. Everything is specified only
in route_lock_glossary and failed_attempt_notes inside evidence.duckdb, so the
folding engine has to be rebuilt from the evidence, not patched from the naive
shipped tool.
