Difficulty Explanation
Agents must harden a railway safety interlock where bound SQL lookups, live per-request relay snapshots, first-match authorization, conditional relay transitions, transition-count ranges, ordered release-sequence automata, sequence-grant voiding when a relay returns to its per-request initial snapshot, prior-station visits, armed lock groups, and cyclic state-space routing interact. A shortcut can visit the same stations and fire the same relay transition without completing the required C-F-C edge sequence, and a recirculation through arrival voids stale clearance until the circuit is re-earned. Authorization must be recomputed for the full state, while equal-length safe routes need deterministic edge-id ordering.

Solution Explanation
Parameterize untrusted station lookups, load relay snapshots afresh for every request, load ordered release sequences and conditional edge transitions from PostgreSQL, and sort rules by ascending priority then rule id. For each search state, evaluate first-match rules—including minimum/maximum transition-count and completed-sequence predicates—and fixed-point armed lock relay before considering edges. After an authorized crossing, advance contiguous sequence progress and relay transitions, void completed sequences when a relay returns to its per-request initial snapshot after firing, then search over station, relays, counters, sequence progress/completions, and visited stations.

Verification Explanation
Tests exercise the live health and planning endpoints across direct, scenic, blocked, clearance, wildcard, conjunction, relay-release, sequence-gated departure, armed-group, overlapping-group, and recirc-checkpoint lineups. They mutate live relay rows between requests, distinguish a true C-F-C release from an out-of-order F-C shortcut, verify sequence grants void on yard return-to-initial and must be re-earned before checkpoint departure, cover transition-count windows, equal-length edge-id tie-breaking, repeated stations, bounded cyclic termination with cycle_guard true, empty unreachable paths, exact seeded routes, and parameterized station lookup source.
