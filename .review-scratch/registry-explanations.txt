=== Difficulty ===
Agents must derive one release decision from a live registry and H2 event histories whose timelines end at different points. Gate 2 calibration stops at the operative run after transitive void propagation, while waiver, approval, and role replay continue to decision time. Eligible waivers then compete across raw failures inside suppression groups, and reciprocal replacements cannot change groups. This forces agents to compose temporal replay, group arbitration, anchoring, authority epochs, and fallback selection instead of translating each control independently.

=== Solution ===
Implement `/app/reconcile-model-release` in Java: fetch registry candidates, derive operative runs after transitive void propagation, and replay each model's calibration only through the earlier of its operative `captured_at` and `decision_at`. Replay waiver, approval, and reviewer-role lifecycles at decision time; reject replacement pairs with different anchors or suppression groups; filter by quorum, authority, timing, and anchoring; retain only the newest eligible member of each suppression group; then perform ordinary per-reason selection. Emit exact reasons, applied waivers, H2 conflicts, and the H2-AUC tie-break, validate the schema, and support alternate JDBC stores.

=== Verification ===
Pytest independently recomputes the decision from the live API and H2 store and reruns the real Java CLI against the shipped store plus six verifier-owned variants. The new combined variant makes a post-run calibration event, two waivers sharing a group across different raw failures, and a cross-group replacement pair interact. Focused partial-fix probes show that decision-time calibration replay, per-reason waiver selection without group arbitration, or group-blind replacement validation each changes the outcome. Exact manifests, reasons, applied waivers, conflicts, and schema validity remain checked.
